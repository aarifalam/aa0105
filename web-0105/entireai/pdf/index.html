1. PDF to Image

What is PDF to Image?

Sometimes you just want to view a page or an entire PDF as an image. Maybe you're creating a presentation, posting something online, or just want a visual snapshot. This is where the PDF to Image feature comes in. It converts your PDF file to JPG or PNG files page by page.

When should you use it?

  • You want to share a PDF page on social media.
  • You need high-quality images from PDF pages for a presentation.
  • You are converting old PDF documents into editable images.

How to use it:

  1. Go to PDF to Image.
  2. Upload your PDF file (drag and drop or browse).
  3. Choose the image format (JPG/PNG).
  4. Click "Convert", wait a few seconds and download your images.
Pro tip: If your PDF file has multiple pages, this tool will give you a separate image for each page. Make sure your PDF file is not password protected; if it is, unlock it first.

2. Merge PDF

What is PDF Merge?

Have you ever had multiple PDF files and thought, "I wish it was just one file"? Merge PDF does exactly that. Combine multiple PDFs into one neat and organized file.

Benefits:

  • Great for reports, assignments or ebooks.
  • Instead of having 20 separate PDFs, it keeps your files organized.

How to use it:

  1. Go to Merge PDF.
  2. Upload all the PDFs you want to combine.
  3. Drag to rearrange if needed.
  4. Click Merge PDF.
  5. Download the single merged PDF.

FAQ:

Q: Can I merge large files?
Answer: Yes! But large files may take a few extra seconds. Be patient.

Q: Does it keep formatting?
Answer: Absolutely. Your fonts, images and layout remain intact.

3. Split PDF

What is Split PDF?

Sometimes you don't need the whole PDF, but just a portion of it. Maybe you need 5-10 pages of a 50-page PDF. That's where Split PDF helps.

How to use it:

  1. Go to Split PDF.
  2. Upload your PDF.
  3. Select the pages or ranges you want to extract.
  4. Click "Split PDF" and download the new file.
Tip: Use this to submit homework or share only a portion of a report. Double-check your page numbers before splitting—PDFs are tricky if you count wrong.

4. Delete PDF Pages

Why delete pages?

Your PDF may have extra pages you don't need: ads, blank pages, or duplicates. Removing pages keeps your PDF clean and professional.

How to use it:

  1. Go to "Delete PDF Pages".
  2. Upload your PDF.
  3. Select the pages to delete.
  4. Click "Delete Pages" and download the result.
Pro tip: Always keep a backup in case you delete the wrong page. Great for quickly editing contracts or reports.

5. PDF Reorder

What is PDF Reorder?

Sometimes the order of pages in a PDF doesn't make sense. Your introduction might be on the end... oops! Reorder PDF lets you drag and drop pages to fix the order.

How to use it:

  1. Go to Reorder PDF.
  2. Upload your PDF.
  3. Drag the pages into the correct order.
  4. Click Save & Download.
Tip: Perfect for e-books, presentations, or compiled reports. It works well for merging PDFs—you can merge first, then reorder them.
